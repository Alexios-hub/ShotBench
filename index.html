Abstract

Cinematography is the fundamental visual language of film, essential for conveying narrative meaning, emotional depth, and overall filmmaking quality. While recent Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have demonstrated strong capabilities in general visual understanding, their proficiency in comprehending the nuanced cinematic grammar embedded within individual shots remains underexplored and inadequately evaluated. This critical gap hinders progress in fine-grained visual comprehension and limits the precision of AI-assisted video generation. To address this, we introduce ShotBench, a comprehensive benchmark for cinematic language understanding. It features 3,608 expert-annotated QA pairs from 3,549 images and video clips, meticulously curated from over 200 predominantly Oscar-nominated films, spanning eight cinematography dimensions. Our evaluation of 24 leading VLMs reveals substantial limitations; even the strongest model achieves less than 60% average accuracy, struggling with fine-grained visual cues and spatial reasoning. To advance this field, we construct ShotQA, a large-scale multimodal dataset with approximately 70k cinematic QA pairs. Leveraging ShotQA, we develop ShotVL by applying supervised fine-tuning and Group Relative Policy Optimization. ShotVL significantly outperforms all existing open-source and proprietary models on ShotBench, establishing new state-of-the-art performance.

Here we show the overview of ShotBench. The benchmark covers eight core dimensions of cinematography: shot size, framing, camera angle, lens size, lighting type, lighting condition, composition, and camera movement.
